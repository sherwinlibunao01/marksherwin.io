# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences land on this page:
- **Local service business owners** (clinics, agencies, real estate, home services) evaluating whether to hire Mark for GoHighLevel/automation work. Non-technical, time-poor, need to trust fast and see concrete ROI (leads recovered, hours saved, faster response times).
- **Technical evaluators** (agencies, potential employers, collaborators) assessing engineering depth — architecture judgment, build quality, not just claims.

Job to be done: decide, within seconds of landing, whether this person can be trusted to fix a specific business problem (missed leads, slow follow-up, manual busywork), then book a call.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio for Mark Sherwin Libunao, an automation/AI systems builder specializing in GoHighLevel, Zapier, n8n, and voice AI. It exists to convert visitors into discovery calls by demonstrating real, working automation builds (including a live embedded Voice AI demo) rather than describing them abstractly. Success = booked calls / contact, and credibility signals that survive scrutiny from both non-technical and technical visitors.

## Brand Personality

Precise, confident, unshowy craftsmanship. Light, minimalist, modern — never dark-mode-default, never generic "AI SaaS" pastel/gradient template. Should feel like a systems engineer's considered work, not a template. Three words: **precise, warm-confident, considered.**

## Anti-references

- The current implementation itself: generic black-on-white Apple/SaaS clone — rounded-full pill buttons everywhere, backdrop-blur nav, soft drop shadows on every card, a literal three-stat hero-metric row (24/7 / <60s / 10+ hrs), identical repeating card grids for both "GHL automations" and "Projects" sections.
- Dark backgrounds / dark mode as default.
- Cream/beige/sand "warm neutral" body backgrounds (the 2026 AI-portfolio tell).
- Gradient text, glassmorphism, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows above every section, numbered 01/02/03 section scaffolding.
- Generic SaaS-purple or navy-and-cream fintech palettes.

## Design Principles

1. Show, don't tell — the Voice AI demo and concrete before/after numbers do the persuading, not adjectives like "expert" or "innovative."
2. Credibility for two readers at once — plain-language outcomes up top for business owners, enough structural/technical clarity for a technical evaluator to respect the work.
3. Light and minimal is a discipline, not an absence of decisions — personality lives in color, type, and composition, not in surface darkness or decoration.
4. One primary action — every path leads to booking a call; secondary paths (Voice AI demo, project detail) support that, never compete with it.
5. No template tells — actively avoid the current site's own clichés (pill buttons, hero-metric row, uniform card grids) when redesigning.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum (body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1). Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Voice AI iframe must have a clear text-based fallback/description since it depends on audio.
